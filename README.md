## About Me 👩‍💻

Hi, I'm **Dhara Patel** — an engineer, researcher, and builder passionate about making autonomous systems that are not just intelligent, but genuinely *trustworthy*.

I'm currently pursuing my **M.S. in Mechanical Engineering (Robotics & Software Systems)** at **UC San Diego**, where I work as a **Graduate Research Assistant** in the [MURO Lab](https://murolab.ucsd.edu) under **Prof. Sonia**. My research sits at the crossroads of probabilistic robotics, control theory, and machine learning — with a focus on **Model Predictive Path Integral (MPPI) control** and **Distributed Gaussian Variational Inference (DGVI)** for safe, real-time multi-agent systems.

Before grad school, I earned my undergraduate degree in **Computer Science**, which gave me a strong foundation in algorithms, systems, and software engineering. That dual background — CS meets Mechanical Engineering — is what lets me move fluidly between writing high-performance C++ controllers and training deep learning models, between designing robot architectures and deploying full-stack AI pipelines.

### Research & Work 🔬

At the MURO Lab, I collaborate with Ph.D. students and researchers on distributed robotics systems, working on problems like:
- **Safe sampling-based planning** using MPPI with CBF-inspired safety constraints
- **Decentralized multi-robot coordination** through Distributed Gaussian Variational Inference
- **Uncertainty propagation** via EKF-based orientation tracking for state estimation

My industry experience includes working as a **Software and ML Engineer at TM Systems**, where I built backend data pipelines and computer vision systems for anomaly detection in production environments. I've also published **two peer-reviewed research papers** — one on emotion detection in text and one on voice recognition for security systems.

### What Drives Me 💡

I care deeply about building robots that humans can trust. Think Baymax from *Big Hero 6* — gentle, aware, and safe by design. That idea of a robot that is not just capable but *reliably good* is what pulled me into this field and keeps me here.

I'm particularly excited about the intersection of **LLMs and robotics** — using large language models to make robots more intuitive to interact with, and using probabilistic methods to make them safer in the real world. Projects like NavAgent (natural language → robot navigation) and SafeRL-Drive (safety-constrained RL for autonomous driving) are direct expressions of that vision.
